## Key Findings: Transformative C-Level Roles

### Patty McCord (Chief Talent, Netflix)
- "Hire adults, not children"
- Keeper test: "Would I fight to keep this person?"
- Eliminated formal performance reviews

### Jeanne Bliss (CCO, Lands' End → Microsoft)
- $100M → $1B growth through CX focus
- "Earn the right to growth through retention"
- Invented CCO function

### Laszlo Bock (People Analytics, Google)
- "HR should be applied science, not art"
- Data-driven hiring: 14-20 rounds → 4 without quality loss
- 100+ current CPOs trace lineage to his team

### Tim Miller (CRO, Stack Overflow)
- $20M → $175M in 4 years by pivoting ads → software
- Revenue alignment = +19% growth, +15% profit
- Unified sales/marketing/CS/ops

### Keith Rabois (COO, Square/PayPal)
- ER triage mindset: fatal vs superficial
- Barrels vs ammunition: adding people ≠ adding throughput
- Radical transparency (board decks visible)

### Tim Cook (COO → CEO, Apple)
- "Inventory is fundamentally evil"
- 100 → 24 suppliers, $400M → $78M inventory in 7 months
- Operations became Apple's moat

### Sheryl Sandberg (COO, Facebook)
- $272M → $118B revenue
- Turned user data into precision ad infrastructure
- "Future of marketing is personal"

### Gwynne Shotwell (COO, SpaceX)
- Make the impossible operationally routine
- Built $10B+ launch manifest

### Piyush Gupta (CEO, DBS Bank)
- "Think like a tech startup, not a bank"
- Worst-in-Singapore → World's Best Digital Bank
- "Making Banking Joyful" as strategic mantra

## Key Patterns from Board Composition Research

### Amazon: Virtual Roles via 16 Leadership Principles
- Principles function as decision-making proxies
- Every employee carries "virtual board members" in their head

### a16z: 5 Advisor Personas
- CEO whisperer, voice of customer, industry expert, functional expert, public readiness

### Chief of Staff
- Founder force multiplier, especially for solo founders
- Role changes "dramatically every couple months"
- Calendar + sounding board + cross-team connector + gap filler

### Fractional C-Suite (Pre-Series A)
- CFO most common, then CMO, then CRO
- Cost control + C-level expertise on demand

## Bootstrap Interview Research

### Sources Used
- YC Interview Questions (78 questions)
- M&A Due Diligence (strategic/business assessment)
- Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick)
- JTBD (Christensen/Moesta)
- Shape Up (Ryan Singer/Basecamp)
- Lean Canvas (Ash Maurya)
- SVPG (Marty Cagan)

### 5 New Question Blocks for Bootstrap
1. **Why Now** — "Почему сейчас? Что изменилось?" (YC, Sequoia)
2. **Past Behavior** — "Как решаешь сейчас? Сколько тратишь?" (Mom Test)
3. **Purchase Timeline** — "Когда впервые подумал?" (JTBD)
4. **Appetite** — "На 6 недель или 6 месяцев?" (Shape Up)
5. **Kill Question** — "Что убьёт проект? Что сломается при ×10?" (M&A DD)

### Mom Test Rules
- Past behavior > hypothetical future
- "Would you pay $10?" = BAD
- "How much do you spend now?" = GOOD
- Compliments = fool's gold
- Commitment (time/money/reputation) = real signal
